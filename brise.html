contexte

Brise is a conceptual brand dedicated to the sale of eco-friendly products. I was given a one-week assignment to define its visual identity and design an initial mock-up of its online sales application.

Brise is an ethical brand focused on the discovery and sale of eco-friendly products.Inspired by the sea, the ice floes, and the purity of water, Brise offers a visual experience that is light, transparent, and soothing. It embodies a new way of consuming: conscious, sustainable, and aesthetic. With recycled materials, a minimalist interface, and an identity rooted in nature, Brise builds a bridge of trust between innovation, nature, and well-being.

recherches et inspiration

Since I did not have precise information about the type of products offered by the brand, I chose to orient the concept toward the sale of objects made from recycled materials, mainly glass. I began by analyzing several brands and applications from the cosmetics sector, with the aim of drawing inspiration from an aesthetic that is organic, airy, pure, and essential. These four keywords form the foundation of Brise’s DNA. This allowed me to build a moodboard around these concepts and begin developing my branding.

Brand design

The brand name was chosen to evoke freedom, breathing, and a pure freshness, in line with its environmental values. From this name, a logo inspired by windmills was created, paired with a fluid typeface subtly adjusted to convey movement and lightness. The color palette, combining the mineral blue of water and earthy brown, reinforces a connection to the elements. The imagery features immersive landscapes that draw the user into an authentic and calming universe, while the product visuals remain clean and minimalist, ensuring a clear and essential presentation.

UI Design

I first worked on the architecture of the application and defined its key screens: the home page, the item list, and the product detail page, framed by an onboarding flow and a checkout process.

Next, I moved on to high-fidelity design: finalized screens with a refined UI, where the branding is consistently reflected through colors, typography, buttons, and micro-interactions such as smooth screen transitions and subtle feedback animations.

An interactive Figma prototype allows users to navigate between screens, go through a session, and experience visual feedback at each interaction. These micro-details add credibility to the concept and help convey what real usage would feel like.
